A dead phone at the wrong moment isn‘t just annoying – it can derail a workday, cancel a trip, or leave you stranded. A good portable charger is the simplest insurance you can buy, but the market is flooded with models that exaggerate capacity or cut corners on safety. The key is matching the battery size to your actual needs: a 10 000 mAh pack easily tops up a phone once or twice and fits in a pocket, while a 20 000 mAh or larger bank can handle tablets, earbuds, and multiple recharges over a weekend. Look for reputable brands with proven power-management chips – Anker, RAVPower, and Belkin all have strong track records. Check that the output ports (USB‑A vs. USB‑C) match your devices, and consider whether you need fast charging (Power Delivery or Qualcomm Quick Charge). Capacity is only half the story: a 20 000 mAh bank that takes 12 hours to recharge itself is a liability.
